Stevie Ray Vaughan

A Tribute to a Blues Master

    The life and times of Stevie Ray Vaughan:

  • 1954 - Stevie Ray Vaughan was born on October 3, 1954 in Dallas, Texas.
  • 1964 - When Stevie was 10 years old he picked up his first guitar, inspired by his older brother Jimmie Vaughan.
  • 1975 - Stevie and a few others formed the band "Triple Threat", which was later renamed "Double Trouble".
  • 1982 - Double Trouble eventually caught Mick Jagger's attention, who invited them to a private party in NYC.
  • 1983 - After his first record "Texas Flood" came out, Stevie Ray Vaughan was voted "Best New Talent" and "Best Electric Blues Guitarist" by Guitar Player Magazine.
  • 1984 - Stevie Ray Vaughan was unexpectedly recongnized by the National Blues Foundation Awards, who voted him Entertainer of the Year and Blues Instrumentalist of the Year.
  • 1985 - Stevie's second record, "Couldn't Stand the Weather", climbed to No. 31 on the charts and went gold.
  • 1986 - Due to his bad cocaine and alcohol problem, Stevie Ray Vaughan collapsed during a concert in Europe.
  • 1989 - Double Trouble's 4th album "In Step" was released.
  • 1990 - After a concert in Wisconsin, Stevie boarded a helicopter for Chicago, due to heavy fog the pilot lost visibility and crashed killing everyone on board.
